What We Tackle

Common Bathroom Challenges We Solve in Edinburgh Homes

Edinburgh's housing stock throws up specific bathroom challenges. Here's what our customers ask us to fix and improve most often.

Dated or Worn-Out Suites

Old avocado-coloured suites, cracked cisterns, and stained baths are no-brainers to replace. We strip out the old and install your new suite cleanly and correctly.

Poor Waterproofing & Damp

Failed silicone, cracked grout, and substandard wet area waterproofing cause damp to penetrate into walls and floors. We re-waterproof properly — and can carry out full leak detection and repair if water has already spread.

Weak Water Pressure in Showers

Edinburgh's water pressure can be inconsistent. We assess your system and recommend the right pump, shower valve, or header tank solution for a satisfying shower every time.

Inefficient Use of Space

Many Edinburgh flats have small, poorly planned bathrooms. We help redesign the layout — moving soil stacks, rerunning waste pipes — to make the most of the available space.

Slow or Blocked Bathroom Drains

Sluggish drains in an older Edinburgh property are often caused by partial blockages or undersized waste pipes. We clear, reconfigure, or replace drainage — see our dedicated blocked drains service for urgent cases.

Wanting a Wetroom

Converting to a wetroom requires correct tanking and drainage gradients. We've fitted wetrooms across Edinburgh and know exactly how to waterproof them for the long term.

Planning Your Budget

What Affects the Cost of a Bathroom Refurbishment?

Bathroom costs vary significantly. Here are the main factors that determine the final price — so you can plan your budget realistically before you call us.

01

Size and Scope

A small en-suite swap costs far less than a full family bathroom conversion. The more fixtures, tiles, and pipework involved, the more time and materials are required.

02

Fixture and Tile Specification

The quality and style of the suite, tiles, taps and accessories you choose affects materials cost significantly. We can work to most budgets and advise on best value options at trade price.

03

Layout Changes

Moving soil stacks, relocating a toilet or repositioning a shower adds time and materials. A like-for-like refurbishment in the same footprint is always the most cost-effective route.

04

Wetroom vs. Standard Bathroom

Wetrooms require additional tanking, gradient screeding, and specialist waterproofing — typically adding 15–25% to the cost compared to a standard tiled shower area.

05

Condition of Existing Pipework

Older Edinburgh properties sometimes have lead or corroded copper pipework that should be replaced during the refurbishment. We'll advise if this applies after our site visit.

06

Electrics and Ventilation

Fan upgrades, shaver socket additions, and spotlights require a qualified electrician. We can coordinate these as part of the project — just let us know at quote stage.

Design Guidance

Planning Your New Bathroom

Not quite ready to book? Here's a short guide to the decisions you'll need to make — so you can come to us with a clearer picture and get a more accurate quote.

01

Layout — Work With What You Have

Keeping your existing soil stack and waste positions in place significantly reduces cost. If you do want to reposition the toilet or relocate a shower, it's possible — but factor in the extra time and materials. We'll advise on the most practical layout during the site visit.

02

Bath or Shower — or Both?

If you rarely use a bath, converting that space to a walk-in shower often makes the room feel significantly larger and more practical. Families with young children usually prefer to keep the bath. We can show you what's possible for your room size and shape.

03

Is a Wetroom Right for You?

Wetrooms work brilliantly in Edinburgh flats and houses when done correctly. They suit people who want a clean, open look, accessibility, and easy maintenance. The key requirements are a solid subfloor to take the gradient screed and proper tanking throughout — we handle all of this.

04

Tiles and Finishes

Large-format tiles (600x600mm or bigger) make a room feel more spacious and have fewer grout lines to maintain. Matte finishes hide watermarks better than gloss. We can source tiles through our trade accounts or work with what you've already chosen.

05

Storage Planning

Consider where you'll store towels, toiletries and cleaning products before you finalise the layout. Recessed niches in the shower wall, vanity units with hidden storage, and tall cabinets all make a bathroom more liveable without adding to the footprint.

06

Heating Your Bathroom

A heated towel rail connected to your central heating system keeps the room warm and dries towels quickly. We can add or relocate one as part of the refurbishment — just let us know at quote stage so it's included in the price.
