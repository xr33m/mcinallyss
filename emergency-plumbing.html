Common Emergencies

Common Emergency Plumbing Problems in Edinburgh

Edinburgh's mix of Victorian tenements and modern new-builds creates its own set of plumbing challenges. Here are the emergencies we're called to most often.

Burst Pipes

Frozen or corroded pipes can burst without warning, causing serious water damage. We isolate the supply, repair or replace the affected section, and check your system end-to-end.

No Hot Water

A failed boiler or faulty immersion heater leaves your family in the cold. We diagnose fast — from pilot light issues to heat exchanger failures — and restore hot water the same day.

Flooding & Water Damage

Active flooding needs immediate action. We stop the source, pump out standing water where necessary, and assess structural damage — preventing costly long-term problems.

Overflowing Toilets

A blocked or overflowing toilet is both urgent and unsanitary. We clear the blockage, repair any faulty cistern components, and sanitise the affected area before we leave.

Boiler Breakdowns

Boilers always seem to fail in winter. We cover all major boiler makes, carry common parts, and can often get you back up and running the same evening — or arrange temporary heating.

Loss of Water Pressure

Sudden pressure drops signal a hidden leak or mains issue. We use pressure testing to pinpoint the fault quickly and carry out a permanent repair — not just a temporary patch.

Immediate Steps

What to Do Before Our Emergency Plumber Arrives

Taking the right steps in the first few minutes can limit damage significantly. Here's what to do while we're on our way.

Turn off the stopcock

Usually under the kitchen sink or where the mains enters. Turn it off to stop water flow and limit damage immediately.

Switch off heating if a pipe has burst

Turn off your boiler and central heating to stop hot water circulating through the damaged section.

Avoid electrics near leaks

If water is near sockets or your consumer unit, switch off electricity at the mains if it's safe to do so.
