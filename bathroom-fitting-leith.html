Leith Property Types

Bathroom Fitting in Leith Homes

Leith's Victorian tenements pose challenges that require experience to navigate well. Bathrooms in these properties are often small, with pipework chased into solid stone walls rather than running in voids. The shared cast iron waste stacks must be connected to correctly — a poor connection causes blockages, noise, and potential leaks into neighbouring flats. Many tenements also retain original lead supply pipes, which we always recommend replacing during a bathroom refit rather than connecting new fixtures to them.

The harbourside conversions on The Shore and Commercial Street often have generous bathroom spaces but unconventional layouts where waste drainage has to be routed creatively around the original structure. The ex-council properties around Kirkgate and Ferry Road tend to have very standard bathroom configurations that are straightforward to update with a modern suite. Whatever your Leith property type, McInally's survey first, advise honestly, and carry out every installation to a high standard — because in a Leith tenement, a poor bathroom fit affects your neighbours too.

What We Encounter in Leith

Common Bathroom Challenges in Leith Properties

Before any bathroom fitting in Leith we assess these common issues. Knowing about them in advance means no surprises mid-project and no nasty hidden costs.

Low Pressure on Upper Floors

Upper-floor Leith tenement flats commonly suffer low mains pressure. We test pressure before specifying a shower pump requirement and recommend the right pump and showerhead combination for your floor level.

Lead Supply Pipes Behind the Tiles

Leith tenements often have original lead supply pipes concealed behind bathroom walls. Uncovering these during a refit triggers a conversation about replacement — we advise clearly and carry out the work as part of the project at minimal additional cost.

Shared Cast Iron Waste Stacks

Connecting new bathroom waste to Leith's shared cast iron stacks must be done correctly. We core and seal connections to the standard required, avoiding the noise and drainage problems that a poor connection causes for the whole stair.

Limited Space in Tenement Bathrooms

Many Leith tenement bathrooms were added retrospectively and are compact. We maximise every centimetre with clever layout planning, compact suite selection, and wall-hung fixtures where floor space is at a premium.

Unconventional Waste Routes in Conversions

The Shore's warehouse conversions often require creative waste routing to reach a suitable stack or soil pipe. We plan this carefully at survey stage to avoid drainage falls that are too shallow — a common cause of slow-draining bathrooms.

Damp and Condensation Issues

Older Leith properties can suffer from poor ventilation leading to condensation and mould in bathrooms. We install extractor fans to building regulations standards and specify moisture-resistant boarding as a matter of course.
