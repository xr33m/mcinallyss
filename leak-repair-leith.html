Common Leith Leak Types

Common Leaks We Fix in Leith Properties

Leith's diverse housing stock produces distinctive types of leak. These are the most common faults we encounter and repair across EH6.

Pinhole Leaks in Lead Supply Pipes

Lead supply pipes in Leith's pre-1960s tenements develop hairline cracks at joints and along the barrel of the pipe. The slow drip saturates the surrounding masonry silently over months. We locate, section off, and replace lead runs with modern pipework.

Cast Iron Waste Stack Seepage

The joints between cast iron pipe sections in Leith tenements are sealed with lead-caulked rope that shrinks with age. Waste water seeps through these joints into floor voids and wall cavities, causing serious structural damp. We re-joint or replace the affected sections.

Central Heating Leaks in Converted Warehouses

The Shore's converted properties often have long central heating runs through thick stone walls, where corrosion and thermal movement cause joint failures. We pressurise the circuit, isolate the failing section, and repair or re-route as needed.

Inter-Flat Leaks Through Tenement Floors

Water from a leaking waste pipe or overflowing bath in a Leith tenement above travels rapidly through the original timber floor structure. Residents below see ceiling staining before the source is obvious. We trace the origin flat-by-flat and fix the true cause.

Failed Push-Fit Joints in Ocean Drive New-Builds

The newer developments around Ocean Drive and Newhaven use push-fit plastic plumbing. These joints can work loose over time, especially in areas with variable water pressure. We test, re-make, and support the affected joints to prevent recurrence.

Underground Supply Pipe Leaks

Leith's older terraced properties often have ageing lead or early copper supply pipes running under garden paths and back courts. A subsurface leak can go undetected for months until a sudden water bill spike reveals it. We use ground microphones to locate the fault precisely.
