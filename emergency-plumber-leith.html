Leith Property Types

Emergency Plumbing in Leith Homes

Leith's housing stock is one of the most varied in Edinburgh. The Victorian tenements that line Leith Walk, Easter Road, and Great Junction Street were built between roughly 1860 and 1910, and many retain their original lead supply pipes and cast iron waste stacks. These materials are now well past their design life. Lead pipes are prone to pinhole leaks and should be replaced; cast iron waste pipes corrode from the inside and can fracture without warning, releasing foul water into wall cavities and floor voids.

The former warehouses and industrial buildings along The Shore and Commercial Street have been converted into flats and offices over the past three decades. These conversions often have complex, multi-floor drainage systems that were retro-fitted into structures not designed for domestic plumbing — making diagnosis of leaks and blockages more involved than in standard properties. The ex-council estates around Kirkgate and Ferry Road feature system-built housing where isolation valves were not always fitted, meaning shutting off water in an emergency requires finding the mains stopcock at the boundary. Our plumbers know all of these property types and come prepared.

Common Leith Emergencies

Common Emergency Plumbing Problems in Leith

Leith's distinctive mix of Victorian tenements, harbourside conversions and ex-council housing creates specific plumbing failure patterns. These are the emergencies we're called to most often across EH6.

Seized Stopcocks in Tenement Stairwells

Leith tenement stopcocks, often tucked in damp communal cupboards, can go undisturbed for decades and seize solid. When a pipe bursts and the stopcock won't budge, water damage escalates quickly. We free or replace seized isolation valves on the spot.

Fractured Cast Iron Waste Stacks

The original cast iron soil pipes in Leith's pre-1970s tenements corrode from inside out. A sudden crack releases foul water behind plasterwork or into the floor void below. We diagnose, section-off, and replace the failed section using modern UPVC.

Low Water Pressure on Upper Floors

Upper-floor flats in Leith's taller Victorian tenements frequently suffer poor mains pressure. What feels like a minor inconvenience can escalate when a poorly pressurised boiler locks out, leaving you with no heat or hot water in the middle of winter.

Lead Supply Pipe Failures

Many Leith tenements and pre-war semis still have sections of lead supply pipework connecting the mains to the property. Lead is soft and prone to pinhole leaks, particularly at joints. We recommend emergency replacement with modern copper or plastic throughout.

Burst Pipes in Warehouse Conversions

The Shore and Commercial Street conversions often have pipework routed through original stone walls or under suspended timber floors. When these pipes fail the water can travel a long way before surfacing. We use specialist leak-detection equipment to locate the source without unnecessary damage.

Inter-Flat Leaks in Tenement Blocks

A leak from the flat above is one of the most common emergency calls we receive in Leith. Water tracks through original lath and plaster ceilings quickly. We locate and fix the source in the flat above, limiting damage to the property below.
