What We Fix

Common Leak Problems We Fix in Edinburgh Homes

Edinburgh's older housing stock is particularly prone to certain types of leaks. Here's what we deal with most regularly.

Dripping Taps

A worn washer or cartridge is usually the culprit. We repair or replace the internal mechanism, stopping the drip and saving you money on your water bill immediately.

Hidden Pipe Leaks

Pipes buried in walls, floors, or under concrete are a common issue in Edinburgh's older properties. Our water leak detection equipment locates the exact source without unnecessary damage to floors or walls.

Leaking Joints & Fittings

Push-fit and compression joints can fail over time, especially in properties with fluctuating water pressure. We replace failed fittings and test the system thoroughly.

Leaking Radiators

Radiator valves and bleed points are a frequent source of slow leaks that can soak flooring and damage skirting boards. We fix valves, replace packings, and reseal — see our radiator repair service.

Boiler & Cylinder Leaks

A leaking hot water cylinder or associated pipework can cause serious damage if not addressed quickly. We safely assess and repair leaks from hot water systems — and can arrange a full boiler service and repairs if needed.

Leaking Shower Trays & Baths

Silicone seals perish and grout can crack, allowing water to seep into subfloors. We regrout, reseal, and where necessary replace trays to stop water ingress — part of our wider bathroom fitting service.

Act Early

What to Do If You Suspect a Leak

If you notice any of the warning signs below, acting quickly will limit the damage and reduce repair costs. Here's what to do straight away:

  • Turn off the water at the mains stopcock if the leak is active or you can't identify the source — this prevents further damage while you wait.
  • Move valuables and electrics away from the wet area — water near sockets or fuse boards is a safety risk.
  • Take meter readings — if you suspect a hidden pipe leak, check your water meter. If it's still turning with everything off, you have a leak somewhere in the supply.
  • Document the damage — take photos before cleaning up. This helps with any insurance claim.
  • Call McInally's — for an active leak, flooding, or burst pipe call 07449 984820 immediately and we'll treat it as an emergency plumbing call-out. For non-urgent suspected leaks, we can book a same-week diagnostic visit.
