Warning Signs

Signs You Need Your Drains Checked

Most Edinburgh homeowners notice the signs of a developing drain problem weeks before it becomes a full blockage. Acting early almost always means a simpler fix and a lower cost.

Water draining away very slowly

A partial blockage is building. Grease, hair, or silt is narrowing the pipe and will eventually cause a full blockage if left. A quick clearance now prevents a bigger problem later.

Bad smells from sinks or gullies

Trapped food waste, grease, or stagnant water in a partial blockage creates unpleasant odours. The smell usually gets worse over time as the blockage grows.

Gurgling sounds from plugholes or toilets

Gurgling means air is being displaced by water trying to pass a partial obstruction. It's one of the earliest warning signs that a blockage is developing downstream.

Water backing up into a sink, shower or bath

If water backs up when another fixture drains, the blockage is further downstream in a shared drain run. This needs professional clearance — not just plunging the individual fixture.

Overflowing outside drains after rainfall

An external drain that overflows in heavy rain is partially or fully blocked with leaves, silt, or roots. Edinburgh gardens in autumn are particularly prone to this — best cleared before winter.

Damp patches near drains or under floors

Persistent damp near a drain may indicate a crack or joint failure in the pipework, not just a surface blockage. Our leak detection and repair service covers this.
