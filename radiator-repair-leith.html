Leith Property Types

Radiator Repair in Leith Homes

The heating systems in Leith's Victorian tenements are often decades old and have rarely if ever had a professional power flush or inhibitor dose. The original small-bore copper pipework carries decades' worth of black magnetite sludge — iron oxide from corroding steel radiators and pipework — and this sludge settles at the base of radiators, causing the classic cold-bottom warm-top pattern. It also builds up in pump impellers and heat exchanger passages, silently reducing boiler efficiency and lifespan.

The ex-council properties around Kirkgate and Ferry Road frequently have older pressed-steel panel radiators with original thermostatic valves that have seized. These feel warm to the touch even at the lowest setting because the pin in the valve body is stuck open. Replacing these valves restores proper temperature control and dramatically reduces heating bills. In The Shore's converted warehouses, high ceilings mean more radiator coverage is needed per room — systems here are often undersized and need balancing or extending. McInally's assess the full system, not just the single faulty radiator, to give you a heating system that genuinely works across all of Leith.

Common Leith Heating Faults

Common Radiator Problems in Leith Properties

These are the heating faults we encounter and fix most often in Leith's tenements, converted properties, and ex-council housing.

Sludge-Blocked Radiators in Leith Tenements

Decades of black magnetite sludge settles at the base of radiators in Leith's older properties. The radiator is hot at the top, cold at the bottom, and the boiler works harder for less heat. A power flush clears the system and corrosion inhibitor prevents recurrence.

Seized TRV Pins Causing Overheating

In Leith's ex-council properties and older tenements, thermostatic radiator valves can seize in the open position. The room becomes too hot, the boiler short-cycles, and energy is wasted. We replace the valve head or full valve assembly as appropriate.

Air-Locked Radiators in Multi-Storey Tenements

Leith's taller tenements create air-locking in upper-floor radiators because there are no automatic air vents and the system was never designed to reach this height efficiently. We bleed, re-pressure, and fit automatic vents at the high points of the circuit.

Leaking Radiator Valves

A pinhole leak or weeping gland on a radiator valve can saturate floorboards and the flat below in a Leith tenement surprisingly quickly. We isolate and replace the faulty valve, check all other valves in the system, and inhibit the circuit to prevent further corrosion.

Unbalanced Systems in Shore Conversions

Harbourside conversions often have heating circuits that were extended room by room as the building was developed. This creates unbalanced systems where some radiators are very hot and others barely warm. We re-balance the entire system for even heat distribution.

Noisy Heating Pipes in Leith Tenements

Banging, ticking, or gurgling from the heating pipes in a Leith tenement is often caused by expansion in undersized duct clips or by air in the system. We diagnose the noise source, apply the correct fix, and advise on insulating pipe clips to prevent recurrence.
