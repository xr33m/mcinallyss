Common Heating Faults

Common Radiator & Heating Problems in Edinburgh

These are the heating faults we're called to most often across Edinburgh homes.

Cold Spots on Radiators

Cold at the top means trapped air — we bleed and rebalance. Cold at the bottom means sludge — we flush the affected radiator or power flush the system if multiple radiators are affected.

Leaking Radiator Valves

TRV and lockshield valve leaks are common in Edinburgh's older properties. We replace the valve packing or fit a new valve to stop the drip and protect your flooring and skirting boards.

Noisy Pipes & Banging Radiators

Banging, clanking, or gurgling heating systems are often caused by trapped air, sludge, or pipe expansion issues. We diagnose the root cause and fix it properly.

Some Rooms Won't Heat Up

Unbalanced systems mean some radiators get all the flow while others get none. We balance your system so every room in your Edinburgh home heats evenly.

Stuck or Seized TRV Valves

Thermostatic radiator valves seize up when not moved for extended periods — especially in Edinburgh properties where heating is turned off for summer. We free, repair, or replace seized TRVs quickly.

System Pressure Keeps Dropping

A heating system that needs constant repressurising has a leak somewhere in the system or a faulty expansion vessel. We track down the cause and fix it permanently — not just top it up.

Warning Signs

Signs Your Heating System Needs Attention

Most Edinburgh homeowners put up with poor heating performance for months before calling an engineer. These are the signs you shouldn't ignore — catching them early usually means a simpler fix and a lower bill.

Radiators cold at the bottom

Almost always sludge. Magnetite and rust settle at the bottom of the radiator and block circulation. Power flushing or a chemical clean restores heat output.

One radiator not heating up at all

Usually a closed, stuck, or seized TRV valve. Easy to fix in most cases — no need to drain the system. We carry common valve parts on the van.

Repeated pressure loss

If you're topping up pressure more than once every few months, there's a leak or a faulty expansion vessel. We find it and fix it — not just refill it again. Our leak detection and repair service covers this.

Banging or gurgling pipes

Banging when the heating comes on or goes off suggests air locks, sludge, or expansion in the pipework. Gurgling usually means trapped air or low system pressure.

Uneven heating between rooms

Some radiators red hot, others barely warm? That's a system balance problem. We set lockshield valves to distribute flow evenly so every room reaches temperature.

Valves that won't turn properly

Seized TRVs stuck open or closed are common in Edinburgh properties where heating is off over summer. We free and lubricate or replace valves that have become stiff or immovable.

Power Flushing

When Is a Power Flush the Right Fix?

A power flush forces clean water through your central heating system at high velocity, removing sludge, rust particles, and scale that have built up over years. It's one of the most effective ways to restore full heating performance in older Edinburgh properties.

We only recommend a power flush when it will make a genuine difference. After the flush we dose the system with a corrosion inhibitor to slow future build-up and extend system life. Where a full system flush isn't needed, a localised chemical clean of affected radiators is often sufficient.

Get a Power Flush Quote

Multiple cold radiators

When more than two radiators have cold spots, the sludge is systemic. Flushing the whole system is more effective and lasting than treating each radiator individually.

Dirty, dark system water

If the water in your system is dark brown or black when you bleed a radiator, there's significant oxidation and sludge present. A power flush removes it thoroughly.

Poor circulation throughout

When the boiler is working but heat isn't reaching radiators efficiently, blocked flow from sludge is usually the cause. Flushing restores free circulation.

Cold spots returning after bleeding

If you've bled radiators but cold spots return within weeks, the underlying sludge hasn't been removed. A power flush solves the problem at source.

Before a new boiler installation

Most boiler manufacturers recommend a power flush before installing a new boiler on an existing system, to protect the new components from contamination.
