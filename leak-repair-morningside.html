Local Property Knowledge

Leak Repair in Morningside Homes

Morningside's housing stock presents leak-detection challenges that are specific to this part of Edinburgh. The substantial Victorian stone villas along Braid Road and Cluny Gardens were built at a time when lead was the default material for internal supply pipework. After a century or more of service, lead pipes develop pinhole corrosion — particularly at joints and bends — creating slow leaks that seep undetected until they cause visible damp or staining.

The conversion flats that now occupy many of Morningside's largest Victorian properties present their own complications. Pipework serving upper-floor flats often travels through the floor void of the flat below. A leak originating in the first-floor kitchen may not become visible until it has spread across the ground-floor ceiling. Identifying the precise source requires careful pressure isolation and acoustic detection rather than guesswork and indiscriminate board-lifting.

Gravity-fed hot water systems — still found throughout Morningside — can also develop slow leaks at cylinder connections, tank outlets, and the distribution pipes that run down from the loft. These leaks are frequently mistaken for condensation until the damage has already spread. McInally's understands every aspect of this unique Morningside housing stock, and we approach every leak job with the care these properties deserve.

Morningside Leak Issues

Common Leak Problems in Morningside Properties

Morningside's age and building stock create a distinct range of leak types. Here are the most frequent issues we resolve in EH10 homes.

Lead Pipe Pinhole Leaks

Original lead supply pipes in pre-1970 Morningside properties develop pinhole corrosion that can weep water slowly beneath floorboards for months. We detect, repair, and replace lead pipework with minimal disruption to original floors and finishes.

Hidden Leaks Through Shared Ceilings

In Morningside conversion flats, a leak in the flat above often travels through floor voids before emerging as a damp stain or drip in the ceiling below. We isolate each section of pipework to find the true source without involving the wrong flat unnecessarily.

Gravity-Fed Cylinder and Tank Leaks

Ageing copper hot water cylinders and header tanks common to Morningside's Victorian homes can develop weeping joints and corroded tank walls. These slow leaks often saturate loft insulation and ceiling joists before being noticed — we locate and repair them cleanly.

Concealed Pipe Leaks in Stone Walls

Morningside's thick Victorian sandstone walls sometimes conceal pipework that develops slow joint failures over time. Damp patches appearing on internal plasterwork are a tell-tale sign — we use thermal and acoustic detection to find the source precisely before cutting into the wall.

Leaking Radiator Connections in Older Systems

Older Morningside heating systems — particularly those not converted to combi boilers — often have radiator connections and valve fittings that have corroded over decades. Weeping radiator connections can saturate skirting boards and floor finishes before being spotted.

Bath and Shower Seal Failures in Converted Flats

Failed silicone seals around baths and shower trays in Morningside conversion flats are a leading cause of inter-flat water damage. Water penetrates tile joints and subfloors over weeks before appearing in the ceiling below. We investigate, re-seal, and advise on any remedial work required.
