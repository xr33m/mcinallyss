Leith Property Types

Blocked Drains in Leith Homes

Leith's drainage is more complex than in areas with uniform modern housing. The Victorian tenements that line Leith Walk, Constitution Street, and Easter Road drain through shared cast iron soil stacks — often 100 to 130 years old — that run vertically through the full height of the building. The joints between cast iron sections were originally sealed with lead-caulked rope that has, in many properties, shrunk and cracked with age. Tree roots from back-court gardens have found their way in through these joints, and the corroded inner surface of the pipe catches fat and paper debris far more aggressively than smooth modern UPVC.

The harbourside conversions on The Shore and Commercial Street drain via systems that were retro-fitted into former industrial buildings. These drainage runs can have shallow gradients — because the floor levels of the original building dictated the pipe route — making fat and food build-up more likely. The newer Ocean Drive and Newhaven developments use modern plastic drainage, but communal ground-floor gullies for these dense developments carry large volumes of waste and can block rapidly if residents dispose of cooking fat down the drain. McInally's approach every Leith drain clearance with knowledge of the property type behind it.

Common Leith Drain Blockages

Common Blocked Drain Problems in Leith Properties

Leith's mix of Victorian drains, harbourside conversions, and dense new-build developments produces specific and recurring drain blockage types. These are the ones we clear most often.

Root Intrusion in Cast Iron Tenement Drains

Tree roots from back-court gardens in Leith's Victorian tenements enter through corroded cast iron drain joints, building up over years into a mass that completely blocks the pipe. We cut and jet the roots out and recommend CCTV survey to assess whether the drain section needs lining or replacement.

Grease Blockages in Shared Waste Stacks

Fat and grease from multiple kitchen outlets sharing a Leith tenement's cast iron waste stack accumulates into a solid plug at the base of the stack or at the first change of direction. High-pressure jetting clears these blockages completely — a plunger merely compacts them further.

Blocked Toilets in Leith Tenement Flats

A blocked toilet in a Leith tenement can back up and overflow quickly, with the water finding its way to the flat below. We clear toilet blockages using specialist rods and jetting equipment, and check the full soil stack for any underlying structural issue.

Slow-Draining Baths and Showers in Conversions

The Shore's warehouse conversions sometimes have drainage runs with insufficient fall, causing waste water to move slowly and deposit soap scum and hair before it reaches the stack. We jet the waste runs and advise on whether the fall angle can be improved.

Blocked External Gullies in Leith Back Courts

The shared back courts in Leith's tenement blocks collect debris, leaves, and rainwater. When the gully drain blocks it creates standing water that saturates the foundations of the building. We jet these gullies clear and install rodding eyes where access is limited.

Collapsed Drain Sections Under Leith Properties

In Leith's oldest streets, the underground drain runs under back courts can have partially collapsed sections where the cast iron has rusted through. CCTV survey reveals these collapses, which cause recurring blockages and can lead to subsidence. We carry out targeted drain repair or no-dig lining as appropriate.
